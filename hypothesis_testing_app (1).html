Decision guide

Z-test (one-sample)

σ knownn ≥ 30

Uses standard normal distribution. Critical value: ±1.96 at α=0.05.

One-sample t-test

σ unknownn < 30

Uses t-distribution, df = n−1. Heavier tails than Z.

Two-sample Z-test

Both σ₁, σ₂ known

Compares two independent group means when population SDs are known.

Two-sample t-test

Two independent groups

Pooled: assume σ₁=σ₂. Welch: when variances differ (use Levene's test).

Paired t-test

Before/afterMatched pairs

Removes between-subject variability. More powerful than two-sample t when applicable.

Key formulas

One-sample Z-test

Z = (x̄ − μ₀) / (σ / √n)

One-sample t-test

t = (x̄ − μ₀) / (s / √n)    df = n − 1

Two-sample Z-test

Z = (x̄₁ − x̄₂) / √(σ₁²/n₁ + σ₂²/n₂)

Two-sample t (pooled)

t = (x̄₁ − x̄₂) / (sp × √(1/n₁ + 1/n₂))   df = n₁+n₂−2

Paired t-test

t = d̄ / (sd / √n)    df = n − 1
